About Me

Hey there! I'm Mohammed,


I trust you're doing well. A little bit about myself: On a 23rd march of 1992, I was welcomed into this place they named as earth, and ever since has been my only home, despite my unmet desire to move to Mercury or Pluto.

Incase you ever get to find-out that I'm a weirdo, you already know, but lets not drift away from the subject.


Eight years ago, I started out in IT as a Hardware and Network Engineer, three years after, I diverted to programming. Now as a self-made software engineer and architect, I'm able to design system software architecture, build systems fullstack-wise with modern frontend tech, and usually a Java backend powered by Microsoft SQL server database.


Over the past year, I've written about a 100KSLOC to build Queue (a webapp that lets people discover medical and beauty services and take line spots to book appointments, and also to view news updates that the businesses post to keep customers informed and engaged). To find out more about Queue, click on Projects link on the top Nav. My current focus revolve around web development with specialty and mastery on bare-bone coding in the various web dev programming languages. By that I mean, I don't easily submit to cooky cutter solutions and frameworks, though I have experience with some frameworks such as React, Java-Spring MVC and others.


I've also taken courses in Database Development and Administration, giving me the ability to analyze, design, implement and maintain databases in MS SQL server with its respective management tool.
