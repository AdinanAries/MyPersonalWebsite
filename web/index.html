Welcome!


Eight years ago, I started out in IT as a computer hardware and networking engineer, and also database administrator. Three years later, perhaps of mostly doing database stuff and writing SQL code, I figured my passion to programming, so I decided to go full-time in the direction of software. Now as a self-made software engineer and architect, I design system software architecture, build systems fullstack-wise with modern frontend tech, and usually a java backend powered by Microsoft SQL server database.

Over the past year, I've written about a 100KSLOC to build Queue (a webapp that lets people discover medical and beauty services and take line spots to book appointments, and also to view news updates that the businesses post to keep customers informed and engaged) Click here to see Queue. As a founder, CEO and project coordinator at AriesLab, I've led my dev teams to substantial wins in various software projects. My most recent main focus has revolved around web development and software architecture with mastery on bare-bone coding in the various web dev programming languages. That means, I don't easily submit to cooky cutter solutions or frameworks, despite being able to do React, Java-Spring MVC and other frameworks. I also have years of experience in database development and administration which equips me with the ability to analyze, design, implement and maintain databases in MS SQL server using its respective management tools.


My aim with this website is to offer free mentorship for beginners who seek to grow in web development, by sharing my knowledge and projects, and most importantly to help those who are on the self-taught route grow and become better developers.

About Me

Whether you're studying CS in college or enrolled in a boot camp, or self-teaching yourself programming, one thing holds true - in all cases, you need to put in hours and hours of work to develop your skills. Software is one of the highly paying industries where a college degree isn't required to land your first job. However, in order to get a programming job, you need to be equipped with specific skills required in the industry. This skills-set comes in the form of trending technologies you need to know, specific CS topics you need to master, and also your ability to understand the tech ecosystem properly.


Hey there! My name is Mohammed Adinan. I'm 26 years old. I started out in IT when I was 18 years old. Over the course of 8 years in IT, out of which I've been doing software for 5 years now, I've become a software engineer and architect after writing and seeing so much code and doing some architecture works as well. As of 2014, I was traveling back and forth from Ghana (where I come from) to London to negotiate on hardware supply deals with London based hardware vendors on behalf of the Ghana Nation Youth Authority (N.Y.A), as I held a two year contract position as regional overall systems administrator and infrastructure coordinator of the NYA.


In 2015, as my employment contract with the NYA was ending that year, I decided that I will establish a startup. Later that same year, I applied as NYA delegate to an upcoming UN Summit which was to be held here, in US. My application was successful, so I flew to NY. As I stayed in NY for some time, I felt it was a perfect opportunity for me to establish my Start-up in NY. I started a small software business called AriSoft which unfortunately didn't succeed. A year later, after months of market research, learning and gathering resources and also making some strategic connections in the industry, I felt prepared to start again. Now as founder, CEO and Project coordinator at AriesLab, I've led my dev teams at AriesLab in many software projects to substantial successes. I've written and seen so much code, designed systems architectures, and managed my teams in technical areas, leading the company to many successes.


Technology has become really complex nowadays, knowing only how to code isn't enough to justify your programmer capabilities. Programmers are expected to know, in addition to being able to write good code and implement effective algorithms, a handful of technologies and also how to use some specific tools in order to fit in the job-market. As a beginner, in order to be able to navigate this complex ecosystem of technologies, you will need some help to identify what you should be learning, the order in which to pick and learn those needed technologies, and how to know when you're ready to start applying to jobs. Having someone who is experienced in tech, who can guide you through this journey is invaluable, I guess.


I offer you free mentorship today, where I will be guiding you through the needed skills like source control, continuous integration, unit testing, etc. I will be sharing some of my projects with you as well in order to give you a better perspective of how software is modularized and how different pieces of code integrate together to become software. We shall also be treating topics like algorithms and big-O notation, as well as data structures.


There's a lot more than I've mentioned. But in all I promise you one thing; you're not alone on your journey to become amazing software developer. Lets connect now and make it easy and fun for you.
